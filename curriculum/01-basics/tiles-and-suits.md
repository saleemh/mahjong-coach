## Bonus Tiles (8 total)

Bonus tiles are not used in forming winning hands but provide extra points.

### Flower Tiles (4 total)
Usually numbered 1-4, each associated with a season:
1. Plum Blossom (Spring)
2. Orchid (Summer)
3. Chrysanthemum (Autumn)
4. Bamboo (Winter)

### Season Tiles (4 total)
Explicitly representing the four seasons:
1. Spring
2. Summer
3. Autumn (Fall)
4. Winter

**Important:** Bonus tiles are immediately revealed when drawn and replaced with a new tile from the dead wall.

## Tile Categories for Gameplay

Understanding these categories will become important later:

### Terminals (Terminal Tiles)
The 1s and 9s of each suit:
- 1-Bam, 9-Bam
- 1-Wan, 9-Wan
- 1-Dot, 9-Dot

### Simples (Number Tiles)
The 2-8 of each suit (middle numbers)

### Honors
All wind and dragon tiles

### Terminal-Honors
Terminals AND honors combined (important for certain hand patterns)

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Tile Counting
- How many total tiles are suited tiles? **108**
- How many total wind tiles? **16**
- How many copies of each unique tile (except bonus)? **4**
- How many total tiles in a complete set? **144**

### Exercise 2: Recognition Drill
When you have access to a physical or digital mahjong set:
1. Separate all tiles by suit
2. Order each suit from 1 to 9
3. Verify you have 4 copies of each tile
4. Group all winds together, all dragons together
5. Practice identifying tiles at a glance

### Exercise 3: Terminology
Practice using the correct terms:
- "I need a 5-Bamboo" (not "5-sticks")
- "That's a West Wind" (not "West Dragon")
- "I have three 8-Dots" (not "three 8-circles")

## Memory Tips

1. **Bamboos:** Look for the bamboo stick patterns; remember 1-Bam often has a bird
2. **Characters:** These all have the è¬ character; learn to read Chinese numbers
3. **Dots:** Count the circles; 1-Dot is usually distinctive
4. **Winds:** Learn the Chinese characters - they're always the same
5. **Dragons:** ä¸­ (red), å‘ (green), ç™½ (white) - memorize these three

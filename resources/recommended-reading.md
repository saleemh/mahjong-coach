# Recommended Reading & Resources

Curated list of books, websites, videos, and communities to deepen your mahjong knowledge beyond this curriculum.

## Books

### For Beginners

**"The Little Book of Mahjong" by Seth Brown**
- Perfect introduction to Chinese Classical rules
- Clear explanations with illustrations
- Covers basic scoring and strategy
- Great as a physical reference at the table

**"Mahjong From A to Zhū" by Scott D. Miller**
- Comprehensive beginner's guide
- American perspective on Chinese rules
- Includes cultural context
- Good for understanding variations

**"A Beginner's Guide to Mahjong" (Various Authors)**
- Multiple beginner books available with this title
- Look for ones specific to Chinese Classical
- Often include tile identification charts
- Useful for visual learners

### For Intermediate Players

**"Winning Chinese Mahjong" by David H. Li**
- Deeper strategic concepts
- Pattern recognition techniques
- Probability discussions
- More advanced scoring

**"The Red Dragon & The West Wind" by Tom Sloper**
- Strategy guide for intermediate players
- Covers multiple variants including Chinese Classical
- Available online at sloperama.com
- Free resource!

### For Advanced Players

**"Modern Chinese Mahjong Scoring" (MCR)**
- Official tournament rules and scoring
- 81 standard patterns
- For competitive play
- More complex than casual play

**"Riichi Book I" by Daina Chiba (Comparison)**
- Though focused on Japanese Riichi
- Strategic concepts transfer to Chinese Classical
- Advanced reading for experienced players
- Understanding different variants helps

### Cultural and Historical

**"The Game of Mah Jong Illustrated" by Patricia A. Thompson & Betty Maloney**
- Historical context
- Evolution of the game
- Cultural significance
- Beautiful illustrations of tiles and sets

**"Mahjong: A Chinese Game and the Making of Modern American Culture" by Annelise Heinz**
- Academic but accessible
- Cultural history of mahjong in America
- Fascinating background
- Not a how-to-play guide

## Equipment Resources

### Where to Buy Sets

**Physical Stores:**
- Asian specialty stores
- Game shops
- Cultural import stores
- Antique stores (vintage sets)

**Online Retailers:**
- Amazon, eBay for variety
- Specialty mahjong retailers
- Import from Asia (often cheaper)
- Check reviews carefully

**What to Look For:**
- Complete 144-tile set
- Clear, readable tiles
- Sturdy carrying case
- Appropriate size for your table

### Quality Considerations

**Budget Sets ($30-50):**
- Plastic tiles
- Good for learning
- Lightweight, portable
- Fine for casual play

**Mid-Range ($50-150):**
- Better quality materials
- Heavier tiles (better feel)
- Nicer aesthetics
- Good long-term investment

**Premium ($150+):**
- Bone and bamboo traditional sets
- Beautiful craftsmanship
- Collector quality
- Heirloom potential

## Critical Evaluation

**When using external resources:**

✓ Verify they cover Chinese Classical (not just Riichi!)
✓ Check publication/update dates
✓ Read reviews or community opinions
✓ Cross-reference with multiple sources
✓ Prefer clear, beginner-friendly explanations

✗ Avoid resources that:
- Don't specify variant
- Have contradictory information without explanation
- Are overly complex for your level
- Aren't maintained/updated

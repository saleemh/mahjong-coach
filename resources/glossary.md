# Mahjong Glossary

A comprehensive reference of Chinese Classical Mahjong terminology. Terms are listed with Mandarin and Cantonese variations where applicable.

## Game Terms

**Mahjong (麻将/麻雀)**
- The game itself; also the declaration when you win
- Pronunciation: Mah-jong
- Literally means "sparrow"

**Hand (局)**
- A single complete game from setup to win/draw
- Typically 5-15 minutes

**Round (圈)**
- Four hands, one with each player as dealer
- Named after winds: East Round, South Round, etc.

**Game (场)**
- Complete session, typically 4 or 8 rounds
- Can last 1-2 hours

## Tile Terms

**Suited Tiles (数牌)**
- The three suits: Bamboo, Characters, Dots
- 108 tiles total (36 of each suit)

**Honor Tiles (字牌)**
- Winds and Dragons
- 28 tiles total (16 winds + 12 dragons)

**Terminals (幺九牌)**
- The 1s and 9s of each suit
- 6 types × 4 copies = 24 tiles

**Simples (中张牌)**
- The 2-8 of each suit
- More flexible for forming runs

**Bonus Tiles**
- Flowers (4) and Seasons (4)
- Revealed immediately, worth extra points
- Don't count toward 14-tile hand

## Set/Meld Terms

**Pong (碰)** - Triplet
- Mandarin: Peng
- Cantonese: Pong
- Three identical tiles
- Can be concealed or exposed

**Kong (杠)** - Quad
- Mandarin: Gang
- Cantonese: Kong
- Four identical tiles
- Draw replacement from dead wall
- Multiple types: concealed, big exposed, small exposed

**Chow (吃)** - Run/Sequence
- Mandarin: Chi
- Cantonese: Sheung/Seung (上)
- Three consecutive suited tiles, same suit
- Can only claim from left player
- Always exposed

**Pair (对子/眼)** - Eyes/Head
- Two identical tiles
- Every winning hand needs exactly one
- Also called "head" or "eyes"

## Hand Status Terms

**Concealed (門前)**
- Tiles hidden in your hand
- Not revealed to others
- Worth more points
- Also called "closed"

**Exposed (明)**
- Sets claimed from discards
- Visible to all players
- Worth fewer points
- Also called "open" or "melded"

**Ready (聴牌)**
- Mandarin: Ting Pai
- Japanese: Tenpai
- One tile away from winning
- 13 tiles forming almost-complete hand

**Waiting (待ち)**
- The tile(s) needed to win
- Also called "wait"

**Shanten (向聴)**
- Number of tiles away from ready
- 0 shanten = ready
- 1 shanten = 2 tiles away from winning

## Action Terms

**Draw (摸)**
- Taking a tile from the wall
- Start of your turn

**Discard (打)**
- Placing a tile face-up in center
- End of your turn

**Call (鸣)**
- Claiming a discarded tile
- Interrupts turn order
- Makes your set exposed

**Win (和)**
- Mandarin: Hu
- Cantonese: Sik/Sek (食/糊)
- Completing a valid 14-tile hand

**Self-Draw (自摸)**
- Mandarin: Zi Mo
- Japanese: Tsumo
- Winning by drawing your own tile
- Usually worth more points

**Pass (过)**
- Choosing not to claim
- Let play continue

## Position Terms

**Dealer (庄家)**
- Player in East position
- Goes first
- Wins/losses worth more
- Rotates after each hand

**East (东 - Dong)**
- Dealer position
- First in turn order
- Special privileges

**South (南 - Nan)**
- To the right of East

**West (西 - Xi)**
- Across from East

**North (北 - Bei)**
- To the left of East

**Player Wind (门风)**
- Your current seat position
- Affects scoring

**Round Wind (场风)**
- Prevailing wind for the round
- Starts as East, rotates

## Wall Terms

**Wall (牌墙)**
- Square structure of tiles
- Built at game start
- Each player builds 18 stacks × 2 high

**Dead Wall (王牌)**
- Last 14 tiles reserved
- For Kong and Flower replacements
- Not drawn during normal play

**Live Wall (牌山)**
- Tiles available for drawing
- Depletes as game progresses

**Break the Wall**
- Determining where dealing starts
- Uses dice rolls

## Winning Pattern Terms

**All Simples (断幺九)**
- Only 2-8 tiles, no terminals/honors
- Also called Tanyao
- Worth 1 double

**All Triplets (対々和)**
- Four Pongs/Kongs, no Chows
- Also called Toitoi
- Worth 2 doubles

**All Chows (平和)**
- Four Chows, no Pongs
- Also called Pinfu
- Worth 1 double
- Must be concealed

**Half Flush (混一色)**
- One suit + honors
- Worth 2-3 doubles

**Full Flush (清一色)**
- All tiles from one suit only
- Worth 5-6 doubles

**Seven Pairs (七対子)**
- Seven different pairs
- Special hand structure
- Worth 2-4 doubles

**Thirteen Orphans (十三幺)**
- All terminals and honors + one pair
- Extremely rare
- Limit hand (maximum points)

**Value Tiles (役牌)**
- Mandarin: Yakuhai
- Pongs of dragons or winds
- Each worth 1 double

**Little/Small Dragons (小三元)**
- Two Dragon Pongs + one Dragon Pair
- Worth 3 doubles

**Big/Great Dragons (大三元)**
- All three Dragon Pongs
- Worth 5-6 doubles (often limit)

## Scoring Terms

**Points (分)**
- Mandarin: Fen
- Basic scoring units

**Doubles (番)**
- Mandarin: Fan
- Japanese: Han
- Scoring multipliers
- Each double multiplies score by 2

**Limit Hand (役満)**
- Maximum scoring hand
- Usually 500-1000+ points
- Examples: Thirteen Orphans, All Green

**Base Points**
- Starting hand value
- Modified by doubles

## Wait Type Terms

**Pair Wait (単騎)**
- Waiting to complete your pair
- Also called Tanki

**Edge Wait (辺張)**
- Waiting for 3 (with 1-2) or 7 (with 8-9)
- Also called Penchan

**Closed Wait (嵌張)**
- Waiting for middle tile (e.g., 4 when you have 3-5)
- Also called Kanchan

**Two-Sided Wait (両面)**
- Waiting for either end of sequence
- Also called Ryanmen
- Best wait type!

## Special Situations

**Dead Hand (死牌)**
- Invalid hand (wrong tile count)
- Cannot win
- Often penalized

**Draw (流局)**
- Game ends without winner
- Wall exhausted
- Dealer may stay or rotate

**Furiten (振聴)**
- Cannot win on discard in some variants
- Occurs when you passed on winning tile earlier
- Can still self-draw

**Genbutsu (現物)**
- Absolutely safe tiles
- Tiles already discarded by opponent
- Cannot complete their hand

**Sacred Discard**
- Similar to Furiten
- Prevents winning on others' discards

## Kong Types

**Concealed Kong (暗杠)**
- All four tiles drawn by you
- Worth more points
- Often shown with 2 face-down

**Big Exposed Kong (大明杠)**
- Calling someone's discard for 4th tile
- All face-up

**Small Exposed Kong (小明杠)**
- Adding to your exposed Pong
- Can be "robbed" if someone needs it to win

**Robbing the Kong (搶杠)**
- Stealing someone's Kong tile to win
- Rare but dramatic!

## Regional Variations

**Hong Kong Style**
- Popular variant with specific scoring
- Often uses animal tiles

**Taiwanese Style**
- 16-tile hands in some variants
- Different pattern values

**MCR (Modern Chinese Official)**
- Standardized competitive ruleset
- 81 scoring patterns

**Cantonese**
- Common in Guangdong and overseas communities
- Uses Cantonese terminology (Sheung, Sik)

## Etiquette Terms

**Ready Declaration**
- Announcing when you're in ready (optional)
- Rules vary by house

**Mahjong Shout**
- Clear declaration when winning
- "Mahjong!" "Sik!" "Hu!"

**Turn Order**
- Counterclockwise in Chinese variants
- Important to respect

- Hu (和): Who

**Cantonese:**
- Pong (碰): Pong
- Kong (槓): Kong
- Sheung (上): Seung/Sheuhng
- Sik (食): Sik/Sek

## Numbers in Chinese

**Characters (萬):**
- 一萬 (Yī Wàn) = 1
- 二萬 (Èr Wàn) = 2
- 三萬 (Sān Wàn) = 3
- 四萬 (Sì Wàn) = 4
- 五萬 (Wǔ Wàn) = 5
- 六萬 (Liù Wàn) = 6
- 七萬 (Qī Wàn) = 7
- 八萬 (Bā Wàn) = 8
- 九萬 (Jiǔ Wàn) = 9

## Dragons

- **Red Dragon**: 中 (Zhōng) - "center"
- **Green Dragon**: 发 (Fā) - "prosperity"
- **White Dragon**: 白 (Bái) - "white"

## Winds

- **East**: 东 (Dōng)
- **South**: 南 (Nán)
- **West**: 西 (Xī)
- **North**: 北 (Běi)
